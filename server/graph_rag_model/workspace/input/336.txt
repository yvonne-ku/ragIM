A 401k account is taxed when you withdraw the money upon retirement. This is similar to how an IRA is taxed. The idea behind this is that you will likely be in a lower tax bracket at the time of retirement. It's important to note that 401k contributions reduce your taxable income for each paycheck, but they do not impact the level of your social security and medicare taxes. Also, the employer match is considered free money. What do you mean by IRA? IRA stands for Individual Retirement Account. It's a type of retirement account where you can delay paying taxes now on your deposits or avoid paying taxes on your earnings later. You can set up a personal IRA for yourself, but not for employees. The investments within an IRA can vary - you can invest in certificates of deposit, stocks, bonds, mutual funds, or any mix of these.
