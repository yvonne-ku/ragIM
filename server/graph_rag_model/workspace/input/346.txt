In the United States, nearly all workers must pay Social Security and Medicare taxes, also known as payroll taxes or FICA. It's important to understand that these taxes are not savings plans, and the money you pay is not explicitly allocated to an account in your name. Instead, it funds the benefits received by current retirees and beneficiaries. When you retire, your benefits will be financed by the contributions of the still-employed workers paying these taxes. It's worth noting that FICA taxes are separate from federal and state income taxes, and as a sole proprietor, you are responsible for both types of taxes. Furthermore, there's a distinction between FICA taxes when you are an employee and when you are self-employed. Typically, FICA taxes are divided equally between the employer and the employee, with each paying half. However, if you are self-employed, you are responsible for paying both halves.
